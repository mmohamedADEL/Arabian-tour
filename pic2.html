Masjid El Azhar مسجد الازهر

Al-Azhar Mosque in Cairo, Egypt, is a historic and renowned Islamic institution founded in 970 AD, serving as both a place of worship and one of the world's oldest centers for Islamic learning

Salah El Din Castle قلعة صلاح الدين

Salah El-Din Castle, also known as the Citadel of Cairo, is a historic fortress built in the 12th century by Salah El-Din, offering panoramic views of Cairo and housing significant Islamic landmarks like mosques and museums

Masjid Sultan Hassan مسجد السلطان الحسن

The Sultan Hasan Mosque in Cairo, built in the 14th century, is a masterpiece of Mamluk architecture, renowned for its grandeur, massive scale, and intricate Islamic design

Masjid Ibn Tolon مسجد ابن طولون

The Ibn Tulun Mosque in Cairo, built in the 9th century, is one of Egypt's oldest and largest mosques, celebrated for its unique architecture, vast open courtyard, and iconic spiral minaret

Khan El Khaleli خان الخليلي

Khan El Khalili in Cairo is a vibrant and historic bazaar dating back to the 14th century, famous for its bustling markets, traditional crafts, souvenirs, and lively atmosphere
